Hometown

Culture

Kediri is a treasure trove of Javanese culture and traditions. The town is renowned for its vibrant arts and crafts scene, including batik and wayang kulit (shadow puppetry). Local artisans meticulously create intricate designs on batik fabrics, reflecting the rich tapestry of Indonesian culture. Additionally, wayang kulit performances are a captivating experience, offering a glimpse into the ancient tales of Javanese mythology and legends.

Reog Ponorogo

One of Kediri's most celebrated cultural events is the annual Grebeg Suro festival, which marks the Islamic New Year. During this festival, locals showcase their artistic skills through colorful processions, traditional music, and dance performances. Visitors can immerse themselves in the rhythmic beats of gamelan music and witness the captivating Reog Ponorogo dance, a mesmerizing spectacle that tells the story of a mythical lion-like creature.

Mount Kelud

Kediri's natural beauty is equally enchanting. The town is surrounded by lush greenery, including the picturesque slopes of Mount Kelud, an active volcano, which offers hiking enthusiasts a challenging yet rewarding trek. The serene beauty of the Wates Lake and the pristine waters of the Pudak River make for perfect spots for relaxation and recreation.

Development

Kediri is not only steeped in history and culture but also strives for modernization. The town has seen significant infrastructural development in recent years, with improved roads, transportation, and educational institutions. With the new addition of Dhoho International Airport, there will be more business opportunities and economical improvement in the years to come. These developments are a testament to Kediri's commitment to balance tradition with progress, ensuring a high quality of life for its residents while preserving its cultural heritage.
